Methodology and Scoring Transparency

This page documents the current model. It is a research prototype and not a diagnostic or medical device output.

Score range 0 to 100
EMA smoothing \(\alpha = 0.3\)
EEG/BCI artifact and biomarker contribution Pending integration

Current Equations

\[ S_{session} = 0.65 \cdot S_{pupil} + 0.25 \cdot S_{recovery} + 0.10 \cdot S_{attentiveness} \]

\[ EMA_t = \alpha \cdot S_{session,t} + (1-\alpha)\cdot EMA_{t-1} \quad\text{with}\quad \alpha = 0.3 \]

Since this is a convex combination, EMA remains within the score range when inputs are bounded. We also clamp stored values to \([0,100]\).

\[ S_{attentiveness} = 100 \cdot \left(1 - \mathrm{clip}\left(\frac{J_{rms}}{0.08},0,1\right)\right) \]

where \(J_{rms}\) is RMS gaze jitter computed from frame-to-frame gaze displacement.

Interpretation Boundaries

1. The attentiveness term currently uses eye jitter only and is intentionally low-weight.

2. Lower jitter can indicate relaxation, steadier fixation, or attention; it is not a pure concentration measure.

3. EEG/BCI integration is planned primarily for artifact rejection and additional biomarkers, not only attention inference.

4. Circadian engagement is modeled from pupil response dynamics and should be interpreted as supportive telemetry, not diagnosis.

Methodology is transparent and versioned in code.
