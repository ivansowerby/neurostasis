Methodology and Scoring Transparency

This is a simple summary of how score is calculated at home. It is supportive monitoring, not diagnosis.

Score range 0 to 100
EMA smoothing \(\alpha = 0.3\)
EEG contribution Active (attention direction modulator)

Simple Scoring View

At home, the app uses three signals: pupil response to light, recovery after light, and alignment between gaze and EEG attention.

Pupil baseline and post-light windows:

\[ B=\mathrm{mean}(p(t)),\ t\in[t_{on}-t_{base},t_{on}],\quad P_{6}=\mathrm{mean}(p(t)),\ t\in[t_{off}+5,t_{off}+7],\quad P_{30}=\mathrm{mean}(p(t)),\ t\in[t_{off}+25,t_{off}+35] \]

\[ \mathrm{PIPR6}=B-P_{6},\quad \mathrm{PIPR30}=B-P_{30} \]

Session score is a weighted combination:

\[ S_{session}= \frac{\sum_i w_i S_i}{\sum_i w_i} ,\quad (w_{pupil},w_{recovery},w_{gaze})=(0.65,0.25,0.10) \]

EEG (\(\alpha/\theta\)-based attention score) then adjusts direction. If EEG attention is high but gaze is off-target, score is reduced and retake may be suggested.

\[ EMA_t=\alpha\cdot S_{session,t}+(1-\alpha)\cdot EMA_{t-1},\quad \alpha=0.3 \]

All scores are clipped to 0-100.

What This Means

1. Higher score means better modeled response for this protocol run.

2. Lower score can come from weak pupil response, weak recovery, unstable gaze, or EEG-gaze mismatch.

3. The system is intentionally lenient and can recommend retake when alignment is poor.

4. This is not a standalone diagnostic tool.

Methodology is transparent and versioned in code.
